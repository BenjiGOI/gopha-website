Scott Mundle

North America Representative

  • Dr. Scott Mundle, a 1983 graduate of the University of Waterloo, Canada began his involvement in organized Optometry early in his career as a Councillor for the Manitoba Association of Optometrists (MAO) in 1986, including being President from 1992-93.
  • Dr. Mundle became the Manitoba Councilor to the Canadian Association of Optometrists (CAO) in 1996 and he served on a variety of national committees, including as Chair of the Publications Committee and the Public Visual Welfare Committee. Added to this, Dr Mundle was the first non-American appointed to the Licensure and Regulation Committee for the American Optometric Association (AOA), St. Louis, MO.
  • Scott Mundle was President of the CAO from 2002 to 2004. He became the Canadian Delegate to the World Council of Optometry (WCO) from 2009 – 2015, President Elect from 2015 – 2017 and President from 2017 – 2019.
  • Dr. Mundle was the WCO Delegate to The International Agency for the Prevention of Blindness (IAPB) (2016 – 2024) and was an Executive Member of the IAPB (2021 – 2023).
  • Married to University of Waterloo classmate Dr. Michelle Georgi, the couple has three children, Cameron, Kirsten, and Kent. On chance that any other free time presents itself, Dr Mundle used to run marathons, but after hip replacement surgery, he has become a road cyclist.

Benjamin Chandra

Research Associate

  • Benji Chandra serves as an Associate Researcher with the Global Optometry Public Health Alliance (GOPHA), where he is currently the global study coordinator for the global optometry education mapping study.
  • Currently living in the UK, Benji works as a Senior University Practitioner at the University of Huddersfield. He combines academic rigour with extensive clinical experience gained in specialist UK hospital clinics.
  • His passion for global public health was cemented during educational initiatives in Belize, Uganda, and India, where he witnessed firsthand that empowering local professionals through education is the most powerful force for sustainable change.
  • In addition to his research, Benji is the Head of Clinical Education at the Global Ophthalmic Institute and actively explores the use of generative AI to enhance education and productivity.
  • Outside of work you can find him going to church, recording a podcast, learning chess, or rock climbing.

Prof. Tuwani Rasengane

Africa Representative

  • Prof. Tuwani Rasengane is Head of Optometry at the University of the Free State and Director of Optometric Services in the Free State Province, South Africa.
  • She serves as Co-Chair of the WHO SPECS 2030 Workforce workstream and is the current interim African regional representative for GOPHA.
  • Prof. Rasengane is a trustee of SightSavers, board member of VOSH International and the Global Ophthalmic Institute, and sits on the editorial boards of leading optometry journals.
  • Her expertise spans child eye health, electrodiagnostics, and visual perception, with a strong focus on advancing global optometric education and public health.

Eva Lazuka-Nicoulaud

Europe Representative

  • Eva Lazuka-Nicoulaud is an international advocate for global eye health and Head of Advocacy and Partnerships for EMEA and APAC at the OneSight EssilorLuxottica Foundation. In her current role, Eva works with governments, institutions, and partners to advance initiatives such as WHO SPECS 2030.
  • At GOPHA, Eva is a co-founding and interim Board member representing Europe. She also serves on the Board of Trustees of the European Academy of Optometry and Optics (EAOO).
  • In 2025, she played a key role in the first Global Child Eye Health Symposium and the inaugural Global URE Forum, fostering multi-stakeholder engagement.
  • Her previous leadership roles include Director of the Vision Impact Institute for Europe and Africa, Global Head of Professional Affairs, Editor-in-Chief of the International Review of Ophthalmic Optics, and Global Head of New Developments.
  • She has contributed several innovative products, patents, and publications, emphasizing vision as a human right and a vital component of health and sustainable development.
  • Based in France, Eva enjoys outdoor sports and running half-marathons.

Rajeev Prasad

South Asia Representative

  • Global Leadership: President-Elect of the World Council of Optometry (WCO), President of the Asia-Pacific Council of Optometry (APCO), and Secretary General of the Indian Optometric Association (IOA).
  • GOPHA Representative: Co-Founder and Interim Board Member, representing South Asia in global discussions on prevention and equity.
  • Extensive Experience: Over 28 years in clinical care, education, and policy advocacy, collaborating with the WHO, Lions International, and Special Olympics.
  • Educational Pioneer: Founded EyeGurukul, a digital platform facilitating 500+ lectures for the optometry community.
  • Advocacy & Impact: Instrumental in establishing the Bihar Optometry Association and organizing screenings for 10,000+ children.
  • Recognition: Honored as Optometrist of the Year (2018, 2020) with service awards from Lions and Rotary International.
  • Professional Focus: Dedicated to low vision rehabilitation, neuro-optometry, and establishing vision as a fundamental human right.

Dr. Hector Santiago

Latin America Representative

  • Dr. Hector Santiago was born in Ponce, Puerto Rico. He has degrees in physics (BS), biomedical sciences (Ph.D.), and optometry (OD), and has dedicated over 40 years to optometric education, serving as Dean at two optometric institutions.
  • He has led over 25 humanitarian clinics in Latin America, providing eye care to over 20,000 patients.
  • He received the Brien Holden Humanitarian Award and is a Lions Clubs International Ambassador of Goodwill.
  • Dr. Santiago has authored over 100 publications, posters, and presentations. He is a board member of VOSH/International and Latinos En Optometry (LEO), and a member of GOPHA.
  • Dr. Santiago is a full professor and research director at the Inter American University of Puerto Rico School of Optometry.

Associate Professor Uchechukwu Levi Osuagwu

Australia Representative

  • Associate Professor Uchechukwu Levi Osuagwu is an internationally recognised public health and vision science researcher at Western Sydney University, Australia. He serves as an Associate Professor of Rural Health at the Bathurst Rural Clinical School, where his work focuses on disease prevention, eye health, and community-driven solutions.
  • Dr. Osuagwu’s research spans diabetes prevention, eye and vision health, mental health, and broader social determinants of health, with a strong emphasis on translating evidence into practical, community-based interventions.
  • He has published over 100 peer-reviewed articles, with more than 86% appearing in high-impact international journals, and has secured over AUD $2.3 million in competitive research funding as a Chief Investigator.
  • A Fellow of the American Academy of Optometry (FAAO), he currently serves as President of the Academy’s African Chapter and as Director of Research and Advocacy for the African Council of Optometry.
  • He is the founding lead of several international research collaborations, including the Centre for Eyecare and Public Health Intervention Initiative (CEPHII), which advances equitable eye care and health systems research.
  • His contributions to research leadership and capacity building have been recognised through multiple national and international awards, including the 2022 Fellowship Award of the American Academy of Optometry.

Hassan Awada

Middle East & Eastern Mediterranean Representative

  • Mr. Hassan Awada is an optometrist and ocularist with extensive clinical and leadership experience across the Middle East and Europe. He holds a Bachelor’s degree in Optometry from the University of Paris and runs specialized ocularist practices in both Nice and Beirut.
  • Working across eight countries in the Middle East, Mr. Awada focuses on advanced eye care, prosthetic eye services, and improving access to quality optometry in the region.
  • He is the former President and current Director General of the Eastern Mediterranean Council of Optometry (EMCO), where he has played a key role in professional development, education, and regional collaboration.
  • Mr. Awada has also served on the Executive Committee of the World Council of Optometry (WCO), contributing to global strategies for vision care and professional standards.
  • In addition, he represents GOPHA (Global Optometry Alliance) for the Eastern Mediterranean region, working to integrate optometry within public health and to address preventable vision impairment on a population level.
